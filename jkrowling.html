J.K. Rowling

J.K. Rowling is a British author best known for writing the *Harry Potter* series, one of the most successful and beloved franchises in literary history. The first book, *Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone*, was published in 1997, and the series went on to sell over 500 million copies worldwide. Rowling's books have not only captivated readers of all ages, but they've also had a profound impact on the world of children's literature. Besides the *Harry Potter* books, Rowling has written adult novels such as *The Casual Vacancy* and the *Cormoran Strike* detective series under the pseudonym Robert Galbraith. Her writing has won numerous awards, and she continues to inspire fans with her tales of magic, adventure, and personal growth. She is also an advocate for numerous social causes, including children's rights and poverty alleviation.

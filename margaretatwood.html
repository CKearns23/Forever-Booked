Margaret Atwood

Margaret Atwood is a renowned Canadian author, poet, literary critic, and essayist. Known for her compelling narratives and sharp social commentary, Atwood has written a wide variety of works that explore themes of feminism, dystopia, and the human condition. Her most famous novel, *The Handmaid's Tale*, is regarded as one of the most influential books of the 20th century and has been adapted into a popular TV series. Atwood’s other notable works include *Oryx and Crake*, *The Blind Assassin*, and *Alias Grace*. She has won numerous prestigious awards, including the Booker Prize, and is recognized as one of the foremost voices in contemporary literature. Atwood’s writing is celebrated for its depth, narrative complexity, and insight into human nature. She lives in Toronto, Canada.

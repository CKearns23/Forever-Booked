Robert Frost

Robert Frost (1874–1963) was an American poet renowned for his deep and evocative exploration of rural New England life. He received four Pulitzer Prizes for Poetry and is often regarded as one of the greatest American poets of the 20th century. Frost’s works are characterized by their simplicity and accessibility, but they also often contain layers of complexity and profound insight into human nature. His best-known poems, such as *The Road Not Taken* and *Stopping by Woods on a Snowy Evening*, have become iconic parts of American literary culture. Frost’s poetry often deals with themes of nature, rural life, personal reflection, and the human condition. He remains one of the most celebrated poets in American history.

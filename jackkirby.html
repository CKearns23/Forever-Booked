Jack Kirby

Jack Kirby (1917–1994) was a legendary American comic book artist, writer, and editor, often referred to as the "King of Comics." He is best known for his pioneering work at Marvel and DC Comics, where he co-created iconic characters like the X-Men, the Fantastic Four, and the New Gods. Kirby's style is defined by dynamic action, bold visuals, and a sense of cosmic grandeur, blending mythology, science fiction, and superhero themes. His work has had a lasting influence on the comic book industry, shaping the modern superhero genre.

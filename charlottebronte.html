Charlotte Brontë

Charlotte Brontë (1816–1855) was an English novelist and poet, best known for her classic novel *Jane Eyre*. Brontë's works explore themes of passion, morality, and social class. In *Jane Eyre*, she created one of literature's most enduring heroines, an independent and resilient woman struggling to balance her desires with societal expectations. Brontë’s writing was groundbreaking in its exploration of female autonomy and emotional depth. Her novels, including *Shirley* and *Villette*, remain beloved for their powerful storytelling and deep psychological insight into characters' emotions.

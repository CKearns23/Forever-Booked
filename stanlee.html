Stan Lee

Stan Lee (1922–2018) was an American comic book writer, editor, and publisher, known for his pioneering work in the comic book industry. As the architect of the Marvel Universe, Lee co-created many of Marvel's most iconic superheroes, including Spider-Man, the X-Men, Iron Man, Thor, Black Panther, the Hulk, and the Avengers. His influence on the comic world is immeasurable, and he played a key role in transforming Marvel Comics into the global phenomenon it is today. Lee's storytelling style was characterized by humanizing superheroes and weaving social and political commentary into his work. He also made numerous cameos in Marvel's films and television series. Stan Lee's legacy continues to inspire new generations of creators and fans worldwide.

Jane Austen

Jane Austen (1775–1817) was an English novelist known for her keen observations on social manners, relationships, and class dynamics. Her works, including *Pride and Prejudice*, *Emma*, and *Sense and Sensibility*, have become iconic in English literature. Austen's novels are celebrated for their wit, realism, and strong, relatable female protagonists. She often critiques the societal constraints of her time, particularly regarding marriage and gender roles. Austen's works have remained widely popular, influencing literature, film, and pop culture for over two centuries. Her legacy as one of the greatest writers of the 19th century endures today.

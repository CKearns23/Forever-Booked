Agatha Christie

Agatha Christie (1890–1976) was an iconic British author, best known for her detective novels featuring Hercule Poirot and Miss Marple. With over two billion copies of her books sold worldwide, she is often called "The Queen of Crime." Christie's works combined intricate plots, clever twists, and memorable characters, making her one of the most successful writers in history. Her famous works include *Murder on the Orient Express*, *The Murder of Roger Ackroyd*, and *And Then There Were None*. Her influence on the mystery genre remains profound, and her legacy as a master of suspense endures.
